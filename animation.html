Video Production / The drop Nov/2016

This short animation film has the theme of imagination. When I was sitting on my balcony, I saw a raindrop dropped inside the cup, and there was where my imagination starts. The raindrop then developed to the world of ocean. This film was produced based on the use of green screen and chroma key. The objects are created by hand drawing to create an organic feeling to the video. The combination of real world and the animation world also gives the audience a feeling of mystery and fantasy.

ROLE: Video producer, Sound Editor, Director

Process / Before Editing Moodboard

The mood board was created for setting the tone and style of the film. From figure 1., the main colour scheme will be cold and mainly focus on blue colour. The style will be organic hand-drawing and watercolor. The reason why I chose to show my movie in this way, because blue is the colour of the ocean, and the watercolour effect will add more dreamlike feeling to the imagination world.

Storyboard

Creating storyboard is one of the most important steps in movie production. In figure 2. the three pictures of the storyboard show the camera angles, perspective, and also helps me visualize the ideas and plan out the shots and scenes, as well as the time control. Also, if there were changes needed to be made, I could always fix it before editing the animation.

handdrawing

Since I wanted to give an organic hand-drawing feeling to the film, I chose use pencil to draw the keyframes of the animation. Each of them I have to draw at least two pictures to create the effect that pencil keeps drawing on the paper. In figure 3. it shows some of drawings of the keyframes.

Process / Post-production video Editing

After transferring all of the images to the computer, I puppet tool on AfterEffects to animate the mermaid, and other sea creatures. For the background, I animated the ocean waves, by using many layers as well, such as the solid layers, colour ramped layer and the fractal noise layer. And for the fish school, I also used particle tools to make each of the drawings of the fish became alive.

Many montage editing is at the beginning of the movie, by matching the compositions (water and the ocean scene). I also used the cutting on action, which is the water drop in the wine glass. And the cross-cutting, which changes the location for each shot to create tension to the scenes. For the transition of each cut, I used dissolve transition most of the time, to make create the illusion and dreamlike visual effect.

For the sound, I applied both L cut and J cut methods to make the seamless transition for each cut. For the background music and the deep ocean creature sounds, I download them from Archive that has the CC license, And I record the raining, the wind blowing and water dropping sound effects.
